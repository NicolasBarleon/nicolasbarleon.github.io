Summary

Hydrogen is a promising carbon-free fuel, but lean or ultra-lean premixed H2 combustion faces ignition and stability challenges under strict NOx limits. JETHPAC develops non-equilibrium plasma (NEP), in particular nanosecond repetitively pulsed discharges (NRPD), to (i) guarantee timely, smooth ignition with minimal heat deposition and (ii) actively control combustion instabilities.

Building on experiments at ETH Z√ºrich and simulations at CERFACS, the project studies canonical channel-flow configurations and realistic combustors to derive discharge parameters that maximize radical production and stability. New phenomenological plasma models will enable affordable 3-D fully-coupled simulations.

Objectives

  • Quantify NRPD effects in H2 mixtures for lean ignition & stability.
  • Optimize discharge parameters for radical generation and NOx minimization.
  • Demonstrate control of thermoacoustic instabilities in realistic systems.
  • Develop reduced/phenomenological plasma models for large-scale 3-D simulations.
