ICHAruS

Developing innovative, ultra-low NOx and flashback-proof H2 combustion devices via electromagnetic (EM) and plasma assistance.

Summary

ICHAruS is an MSCA Doctoral Network that advances the understanding and control of H2 flames under EM conditioning and plasma discharges. The program trains early-stage researchers through a collaboration between academia and major European gas-turbine manufacturers, combining experiments and high-fidelity simulations.

The goal is to enable zero-carbon energy and transport by stabilizing H2 flames, reducing NOx emissions, and preventing flashback through EM/plasma-assisted control.

Research Objectives

  • O1: Study EM field effects on species transport & kinetics in H2 (air/O2), incl. pollutant formation and differential diffusion vs EM drift.
  • O2: Develop turbulence-combustion models and LES closures for low/high-energy EM/plasma assistance.
  • O3: Experimental & numerical investigation of EM-assisted control for practical flames (single-swirl and annular configurations).
